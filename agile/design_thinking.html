Design Thinking & UX Research

Course outline

Learn more

Design Thinking is an essential skill for UX Researchers, Designers and Product Teams in general. The interesting thing is you already design. This course will help you articulate the building blocks of your already innate abilities into skills that enable teams to build great products that delight customers and help businesses achieve desirable outcomes. In this course, you will learn the Human-centred approach to making great products and helping teams align on the right culture and leadership to get the job done.

Skills you will learn

At the end of this course, you should be able to;

Transform an idea into great product

Use Low to high fidelity tools to bring design ideas to life

Identify users of a product and their problems

Conduct usability testing

Conduct insightful user research and identify assumptions

Make a plan for product implementation

Design a solution, test for viability and de-risk the product

Support delivery and marketing teams with User Experience Insights.

Who should enrol

“Everyone designs who devises courses of action aimed at changing existing situations into preferred ones.” - Herbert Simon

Design thinking is the mindset that aims to improve the situation of people through the experiences they have. If you're interested in solving problems for people, then you can practice Design Thinking.

You should enrol on this course if you are interested in..

  • Solving problems for people?
  • Learning how to transform an idea into sellable products
  • Curious about how to make products that delight the customer?
  • Curious about how to know which products have a high chance of success in the market before it is built?
  • Interested in helping product delivery and marketing teams excite customers ?

Course Outline

  • Introduction to User Experience, Innovative Process & Design
  • Design Thinking & Innovation
  • Intersection between Design Thinking & User Experience Research
  • Problem Vs Solution Space
  • User Personas & Market Sizing
  • Identify your users and their problems
  • Recognise your assumptions (Actual vs Perceived Problems)
  • Designing Solutions
  • Using Low & High Fidelity Design Techniques to create prototypes
  • Conducting usability tests
  • Supporting delivery and marketing teams with UX insights
  • Designing Product Launch and Go-to-Market

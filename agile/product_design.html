Product Design & Development

Course outline

Learn more
Courses Skills you will learn Takeaway Who Should Enroll Request information ENROL NOW

Great designs is a source of competitive advantage for most companies. Some of the best companies in the world have the best product designs. High-growth companies understand that they cannot underestimate the value of great design. This is why Product Design and UX research are some of the most sort after skills in the Product Delivery industry today. This course will help you develop both fundamental and advanced skills you need to succeed and grow a career in Product Design. It will provide you with the unique understanding to develop customer-centric products from concept and design to production. The lessons will bring together hands-on learning in product design and product development with product lifecycle management. You will walk-through interdisciplinary, collaborative group projects that will require you to apply all stages of the visual design process, you'll develop creative design problem-solving abilities and strategies.

Skills you will learn

At the end of this course, you should be able to;

Master a user-centered approach to design and product development

Conduct design analysis and verification processes to test and validate your design

Back up design decisions with with strategic analysis and psychology

Use graphic design and rapid prototyping tools to present your design

Who should enrol

“Everyone designs who devises courses of action aimed at changing existing situations into preferred ones.” - Herbert Simon

Design thinking is the mindset that aims to improve the situation of people through the experiences they have. If you're interested in solving problems for people, then you can practice Design Thinking.

You should enrol on this course if you are interested in..

  • Are you interested in solving problems for people?
  • Are you curious about how to make products that delight the customer?
  • Are you curious about how to know which products have a high chance of success in the market before it is built?
